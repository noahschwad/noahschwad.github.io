Smile Tiles

I was reading Edward Tufte's book The Visual Display of Quantitative Information and I was intrigued by the idea of Chernoff Faces. If you're not familiar, Chernoff Faces are a quirky way of visualizing multiple variables at once by drawing a face based on that data. A variable is mapped to the shape of the eyes, slant of the eyebrows, shape of the head, etc, and because humans are particularly good at recognizing faces, differences in the data are easy to discern. These faces, however, do not represent any meaningful data and are generated randomly!
