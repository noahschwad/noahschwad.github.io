PhET Interactive Education Tools

I illustrated user scenarios created during a two-day human-computer interaction conference. These were all created on the spot!

Illustration

Multimodal Field Trip

We were interested in imagining how learning technologies could move outside the conventional classroom and connect students to other physical environments in their broader communities. The scene shows a physical and digital environment side-by-side, and the idea is that learners draw upon features of each environment to enrich the other. Both the physical and digital environments are inclusively designed to allow diverse learners to fully participate and work together.

Accessible Theatre Cookbook

We aim to create a Cookbook to support accessible collaborative creation, performance, and enjoyment of accessible multimodal theatrical experiences. New and modified plays would empower actors and musicians of all abilities to perform and provide multimodal presentations for the audience. The cookbook would be mobile and web accessible, or even embedded in the physical environment.

Multimodal Jungle Game

This immersive experience exercises open ended investigation through all senses, creating a way to create, explore, and collaborate beyond current hegemonic user interface implementations which limit and constrain the way we interact with the world. The goals of this scene are largely open-ended with limited structure, but an “escape room” like gameplay helps encourage problem solving, teamwork, and interaction with a player’s surroundings.
